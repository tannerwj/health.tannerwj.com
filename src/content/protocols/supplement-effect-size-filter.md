Layne Norton's [supplement tier discussion](https://biolayne.com/podcasts/dr-layne-norton-podcast/supplement-series-tier-1-episode-22/) puts fundamentals before supplements. Creatine and protein have relatively higher-confidence roles in appropriate training and nutrition contexts.

Fat-loss supplements usually have small effects compared with adherence and energy balance. Sponsorship and product copy are reasons to ask about evidence quality and effect size before treating a claim as decisive.

Norton's [effect-size post](https://x.com/BioLayne/status/2054964281842569358) and Huberman's [behaviors-before-compounds post](https://x.com/hubermanlab/status/2063326890421813260) land on the same filter from different directions: ask whether a result is both credible and large enough to matter after the basics are in place.
