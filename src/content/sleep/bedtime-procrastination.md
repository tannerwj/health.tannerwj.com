Matthew Walker's [post on bedtime procrastination](https://x.com/sleepdiplomat/status/2059718783850430784) names a common mismatch: someone intends to sleep but keeps delaying the opportunity. That is different from insomnia, where the opportunity exists but sleep remains difficult.

The distinction matters because the response differs. A practical barrier, habit, or need for unstructured evening time is not automatically a sleep disorder; persistent trouble falling or staying asleep may need a different approach.
