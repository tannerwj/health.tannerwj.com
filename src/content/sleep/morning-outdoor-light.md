Huberman Lab's [sleep toolkit](https://www.hubermanlab.com/newsletter/toolkit-for-sleep) frames outdoor light early in the day as a useful signal for circadian timing. FoundMyFitness' [Satchin Panda conversation](https://www.foundmyfitness.com/episodes/satchin-panda-3) adds context for why timing of light, food, and daily routine can matter.

Huberman also makes the point directly in a [post on circadian entrainment](https://x.com/hubermanlab/status/1737627073626407105). The durable idea is to treat morning light as a timing cue, not as a precise dose or indoor-lamp rule that applies the same way to every person.
